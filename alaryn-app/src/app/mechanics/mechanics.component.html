Mechanics

Reasonings

My main motivation for making this world was to create a world that I can truly call my own. It is not my first attempt at it, but it is the one I've spent the most effort at. It was made to play Dungeons and Dragons Fifth Edition, so it has not been my intention to make a world that is highly conflicting with the ruleset that I enjoy to play. I've made a world that is not traditionally D&D like, but mechanically there should be few changes, at least when it comes to races and classes. The major changes are in relation to the world, how the different races interact and how the classes get their powers, but it's all nearly all flavour and fluff. In general; if something wouldn't make sense with the world I have made, I will find a way for it to make sense!

In short:

Races allowed: From PHB, all except for half elf. From Volo, Aasimar, Firbolg, Goliath, Kenku, Lizardfolk, Tabaxi, Orc, Kobold. Subraces allowed: All except for winged subraces and deep gnomes. Classes: All allowed, just keep in mind how your character got their power, especially in regards to magic. Feats: All allowed Multiclassing: Allowed Backgrounds: All allowed Alignments: All allowed, just make sure that your character is able to work in a team with others without intentionally messing up for them.

Races

D&D is very diverse in terms of the races that you can play. It has not been my intention to remove races for the sake of removing them, but there are some races that I have not allowed to be used by players. Races that get innate magic will instead learn this magic from harnessing crystals made for them. How the players want to flavour these crystals and how they acquired this is up to them. In the following list I will explain the different races, starting with the Player's Handbook races and then the Volo races:

    Player's Handbook

  • Dwarves.
    Dwarves is one of the major races in this region of the world and all subraces are allowed.
  • Elves
    The elves are nearly extinct and would be a pretty rare sight. They do have some settlements far away, but the details of those locations are unknown to the current setting. Most elves reside in Panther's Rest, and all subraces are allowed. Elves live up to 250 years.
  • Halflings
    Halflings are allowed to be picked mechanically, but lorewise they belong to the same race as the gnomes. Lightfoot halflings are those who lived with the orcs, stout with the dwarves. There is no halfling language and halflings instead learn Gnomish.
  • Humans
    The humans are extinct, but they have returned as the undead Aman.Both standard and variant human are allowed, but player Aman can not have been "born" within the walls of Alzar. They would most likely hail from Al'Ruv, but other options can be explored and worked on with me. Humans know Common and Aman. Aman do not need to eat or drink to live, but they do need to rest, where they enter a stupor like state. Even though they are undead they are still susceptible to magical diseases.
  • Dragonborn
    Dragonborn are allowed mechanically, but they are the mutant versions of the Vulkian lizardfolk. They do not know Draconic, and instead know Vulkian.
  • Gnomes
    The gnomes have more insect like traits described in the races page. They do not look like half bugs or anything like that, but they are still distinct traits. The player can choose to give them wings, but they will not be able to fly with them. Forest gnomes are the gnomes who lived with the orcs, rock gnomes with the dwarves. Deep gnome is not allowed. Gnomes live up to 300 years.
  • Half-Elves
    Half-elves are not allowed. Humans having lost their blessing and having been gone for centuries together with the elves being almost extinct and driven far away makes it so no half-elves are alive, nor can any more be created.
  • Half-Orcs
    The orcs is one of the major races of the region. The Player's Handbook half-orc is used for these orcs.
  • Tieflings
    The tieflings are known as the Azzinth. The Azzinth do not know infernal and instead know Azzinth.

    • Volo's Guide to Monsters

  • Aasimar
    The aasimar are exceedingly rare, but all subraces are allowed. Aasimar do not know Celestial and instead know a language of their choice.
  • Firbolg
    Firbolg are allowed, but rare, at least outside Panther's Rest. They do not know Elvish and instead can choose a language of their choice.
  • Goliath
    Goliath are allowed mechanically, but they are the mountain orcs. They do not know Giant and instead know Orcish.
  • Kenku
    The kenku are known as the Vulkian birdfolk. They do not know Auran and instead know Vulkian.
  • Lizardfolk
    The lizardfolk are the Vulkian lizardfolk. They do not know Draconic and instead know Vulkian.
  • Tabaxi
    The tabaxi are known as the Vulkian catfolk. They know Common and Vulkian.
  • Triton
    Triton are not allowed.If they exist they are not known in this region.
  • Bugbears
    Bugbears are not allowed. Bugbears exist but they do not have good enough relations to be allowed for players.
  • Goblins
    Goblins are not allowed. Goblins exist but they do not have good enough relations to be allowed for players.
  • Hobgoblins
    Hobgoblins are not allowed. Hobgoblins exist but they do not have good enough relations to be allowed for players.
  • Orcs
    The orcs is one of the major races of the region. While the Player's Handbook half-orc is used for most orcs, but some of the more tribal orcs would fit this race.
  • Yuan-ti Pureblood
    Yuan-ti Pureblood are not allowed. If they exist they are not known in this region.

Classes

All classes are allowed. Most classes have been explained in the classes page and should not be affected mechanically. If you wonder how a class functions in my world and it is not explained in the classes page feel free to ask me.
The classes that are able to use real magic are the Aman, warlocks or clerics who worship Levyn, God of Light and Life, or Zuvek, goddess of Death and Secrets. Keep in mind that those who use real magic are highly mistrusted or disliked.

Languages

Languages are somewhat changed from the standard languages. Two new languages have been added. Vulkian is the language spoken by the races from the Vulkian Archipelago. It's a rough language with sharp sounds that can be difficult to learn for non-natives. It was mostly a spoken language and they had a crude written language, but after they made contact with the other races they adopted the common script. Azzinth is the language spoken by the Azzinth. It's in many ways similar to Infernal, but over many years it has changed. Someone who speaks Azzinth might be able to understand Infernal if succeeding at a Intelligence check.
